What do we need from your side:

1) Photo - passport size photograph
2) Address Proof - Latest Bank statement/ Utility bill in the name of director which should not be older than two months
3) ID Proof - Scanned copy of PAN Card of all directors and Aadhar card/ Voter ID/ Passport/ Driving License
4) Registered Office Proof - No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the owner, Utility bill (should not be older than two months) and Notarized Rent agreement (in case of rented property)/ Registry Proof or House Tax Receipt (in case of owned property).

Financial Documents



Financial documents are vital tools for analysing the success of a business. If everything is going well, founders will be asked for their financial documents. These should cover a few aspects of the business, from the revenue forecasts to the operational expenses to the company’s cash flow.
The complexity of these documents can range from a single slide in your pitch deck showing some baseline guesses on where revenues will come from, to highly complicated Excel document that involve macros and formulas changing outcomes based on key assumptions and scenarios.

For general purposes you’ll need to cover at least a few aspects of your financial picture.

Every meeting with an investor will eventually always boil down to the numbers of the business, where a lot of founders struggle to accurately showcase what their plans for the business are and where they see it going.
It is a must to be accurate in these documents and to highlight key figures and showcase what is really important. Investors can find the smallest of flaws or inaccuracies and that could be the reason of a deal going wrong.
